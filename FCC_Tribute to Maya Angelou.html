Maya Angelou

Phenomenal Woman

Pretty women wonder where my secret lies.

I’m not cute or built to suit a fashion model’s size

But when I start to tell them, They think I’m telling lies.

I say,

It’s in the reach of my arms,

The span of my hips,

The stride of my step,

The curl of my lips.

I’m a woman Phenomenally.

Phenomenal woman, That’s me.

Marguerite Annie Johnson was born in St. Louis, Missouri, on April 4, 1928. Angelou attended George Washington High School in San Francisco and studied dance and drama at the California Labor School.

Encouraged by her managers, Johnson began to address herself professionaly as Maya Angeleou and joined the Harlem Writers Guild, where she met James Baldwin and other important writers.

I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings is Ms. Angelou’s first autobiography. She would complete an additional five throughout her career. Maya Angelou was the first black woman to have a screenplay, Georgia, Georgia, produced in 1972. Angelou was invited to recite a poem at President Bill Clinton's inauguration, where she read "On the Pulse of Morning."

Throughout her career Maya Angelou was awarded over fifty honorary degrees, as well as the National Medal of Arts by President Clinton, and the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama. Ms. Angelou was awarded the Literarian Award in 2013.

Maya Angelou passed away May 28, 2014, at the age of 86 in her home in Winston-Salem, North Carolina.
